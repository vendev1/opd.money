What is opd.money?

opd.money is a Sharia-Compliant decentralised lending and borrowing protocol that combines the principles of Islamic finance with decentralised technology. It enables individuals to engage in lending and borrowing activities without traditional intermediaries while strictly adhering to Sharia law.


With every update and release of new versions, previous versions of the protocol will function permanently, with 100% uptime, provided the continued existence of the Venom blockchain. If you want to see more Venom tokens available for borrowing, please drop us a line on our Twitter.

Why Sharia-compliant?

The protocol prohibits interest (riba) and promotes fair profit-sharing and risk-sharing. By leveraging blockchain, opd.money provides a secure and transparent environment for participants to access financial services while upholding their religious beliefs. At the same time, opd.money introduces Sharia-Compliant DeFi and Islamic finance to more people around the world as it (Islamic finance) is often considered more fair and equitable compared to traditional finance due to its adherence to principles such as justice, transparency, and risk-sharing.

How does opd.money work?

To create a new loan request, provide liquidity to existing loan requests, or see your existing deals, head over to the interface and connect a Venom wallet.
Learn more about Venom wallets here.

What's the cost?

Interacting with the protocol requires transactions and so transaction fees for Venom Blockchain usage, which depend on the network status and transaction complexity. There are no additional fees or Service Fees other than the network transaction cost.

Is it safe to use it?

We have tried to minimise the risk as much as possible - the protocol code is public and open source, which allows anyone to interact with a user interface client or directly with the smart contracts on the Venom network.

How to borrow currency?

Go to the Borrow section. Create a new loan request by clicking the + New loan button. You'll see a pop–up with information to be filled or chosen from dropdown fields.
The pop-up is divided into three categories: Loan, Collateral and Optional.


· Loan (all fields are mandatory):
Asset stands for the currency you want to borrow.
The amount is simply how many tokens of chosen currency you need to borrow.
Loan term your loan duration or how long do you plan to keep the currency?
Reward feature allows users to set a fixed amount of tokens as a reward for the liquidity provider in a loan transaction. The larger the reward amount, the more enticing and attractive the user's loan request will be compared to other requests. With P2P lending, users have the flexibility to incentivise liquidity providers with higher rewards, making loan requests more competitive and appealing in the system.


· Collateral.* Simply put it is insurance for the liquidity provider, if a borrower won't pay back the loan:
Asset stands for currencies you can choose as collateral.
Amount - how many tokens of a collateralised asset you are offering.


· Optional. As the name says, you don't have to. The system offers to make a decision if the request will be hanging there forever or just for some time.


* In Sharia finance, loan collateral plays a significant role in securing loans and mitigating risks. While Sharia-compliant financing follows principles that prohibit interest (Riba), the concept of collateral is recognised and utilised within the Islamic financial framework.

How does lending work?

For lending tokens, go to the Lend section. Choose a loan request from the list and click on it. From the pop-up window, click on the Lend button, and confirm the transaction from your wallet.

What is "Deals"?

List of existing users' lendings and borrowings. As a lender you can use collateral withdrawal from outdated deals, as a borrower you can repay loans.

How to cancel my loan request?

Click on a field with the loan request and then on a Cancel request button in the appeared pop-up window.

Where are funds stored?

All funds are stored in a smart contract. The smart contract code is public, open source and formally verified.
